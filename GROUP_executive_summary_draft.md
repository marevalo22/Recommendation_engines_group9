# Executive Summary — Football Transfer Recommender (Synthetic-Augmented Draft)

This project builds an end-to-end football transfer recommender where **clubs are users** and **players are items**. The original project already implemented the four required recommender types — non-personalized, collaborative filtering, content-based, and context-aware — on consolidated Top-5 league data from 2018-19 to 2023-24. The main weakness after inspection was not the overall architecture, but the limited richness of the player, team, and team-player feature space.

To address that gap, the project now adds a **transparent synthetic feature layer** across three levels. First, a player layer creates role-aware latent traits and synthetic observables such as xG, xA, progression, defending, aerial profile, availability, and upside. Second, a team layer creates interpretable tactical style variables such as possession orientation, directness, pressing intensity, width, tempo, territorial dominance, and tactical archetypes. Third, a relational pair layer creates explicit fit variables for role fit, style fit, squad need, affordability, readiness, adaptation, upside, value-for-money, and tactical causality.

The evaluation is deliberately split into separate tracks. The **real-only benchmark** remains the honest baseline. In the reproduced real-only benchmark, collaborative filtering is the strongest model on ranking quality, with **NDCG@10 = 0.0083**, while context-aware reaches **0.0072** and content-based remains weak at **0.0019**. The synthetic base track then shows what happens when richer football vectors are added without label-informed uplift. In that setting, content-based improves materially to **NDCG@10 = 0.0109**, and context-aware improves to **0.0123** with a positive context delta. Finally, the **label-informed synthetic augmentation for proof-of-concept / scenario modelling** produces the strongest PoC result, with the context-aware recommender reaching **Precision@10 = 0.0161**, **Recall@10 = 0.0485**, and **NDCG@10 = 0.0363**.

The most important interpretation is that the synthetic PoC result is **not** presented as unbiased real-world generalisation. It is a transparent scenario-modelled demonstration of how much stronger a transfer recommender becomes when it has richer tactical, role, and pairwise-fit information. The real-only benchmark remains the core evaluation. The synthetic layer strengthens the business case, the named-club demos, and the explanatory power of the recommendations, while the audit files make explicit what is real, what is engineered, what is synthetic, and what is label-informed.
